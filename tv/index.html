What is this?

The OMDb API key lets the tracker pull richer show info — IMDb ratings, posters, and plot summaries — from the Open Movie Database. It's completely optional: without a key the app still works and uses TVMaze for metadata instead.

How to get a free key

  1. Open the OMDb API key page (button below).
  2. Choose the FREE plan — 1,000 requests per day.
  3. Enter your email and submit the form.
  4. Check your inbox and click the activation link (the key won't work until you do).
  5. Copy the key from the email and paste it below.

How this tracker works

1. Your account & privacy

Everything lives only in this browser on this device — nothing is uploaded to a server. Each person has their own login, and each login keeps its own separate show list. To use the same account elsewhere you can sync directly between your devices (see below), or open ⚙️ Settings → Download my data and Restore it on the other device as a manual backup.

2. Import your shows

In TV Time, go to Settings → Export your data and you'll receive a .zip by email. Click Import TV Time Export and pick that .zip (or the extracted CSV files). Your followed shows and watch progress load instantly.

3. Track what you watch

Each show card shows your progress. Use ➕ / ➖ to add or remove one episode, 🎬 to catch up on everything that has aired, or open a show to tick individual episodes and mark whole seasons watched. Episodes that haven't aired yet stay locked. Click any card to open its season list.

4. Posters & upcoming episodes

The app fetches posters, descriptions and next-air-dates from TVMaze (free, no key). Optionally add a free OMDb API key for IMDb-sourced art and ratings. Use Refresh Metadata if something looks out of date. Open a show and tap its poster to view it full‑size.

5. Sync your phone & PC

Tap 📲 Sync device (on the main page or in ⚙️ Settings) to pair two devices once by scanning a QR code. After that, whenever both have the tracker open they sync directly device‑to‑device — on the same Wi‑Fi your data never leaves your network, and it’s end‑to‑end encrypted. Changes are merged automatically (newest edit per show wins), so ticking episodes on your phone shows up on your PC when you get home. It syncs while both apps are open; a closed tab can’t sync.

6. Install it like an app

On mobile or desktop you can add it to your home screen (look for 📱 Install App or your browser's "Add to Home Screen"). It then works offline with your cached data.
